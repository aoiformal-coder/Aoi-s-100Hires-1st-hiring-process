```text
We’ve audited over £50M in B2B Google Ad spend.

And the reality is… 90% of accounts are making the same mistakes.

Here are the big ones we see again and again:

1. Incorrect Tracking

In an ideal world, your only primary conversion is the offline CRM data sync of won deals.

In reality, if your buying cycle is 3+ months, it’s best to use a lifecycle progression (SQL / Open Deal), making sure the lead has been manually reviewed against ICP before re-uploading.

You can also have the ‘fallback’ cookie-based conversion goal in case the Conversion API decides to freak out (which it likes to do).

2. Wrong Bidding Model

Max Conversions or Target CPA are often used without proper tracking in place. 

Start manual, get close to the account, monitor impression share, and then move into automated strategies once you actually have reliable data.

3. Wrong Keywords

In B2B, ‘informational’ terms can masquerade as ‘transactional’ - so we end up blowing a whole load of budget on terms that have zero intent.

To make things harder, the match types continue to get looser, to the point where exact-match KWs have started producing search terms closer to what we used to expect from phrase.

Negative KW lists are your best friend.

4. Poor Ad Copy

This is a funny one - ad copy is literally the only thing prospects actually see... yet most people spend five minutes on it.

Strong ads should highlight differentiators, handle objections, and qualify prospects.

Stay away from abstract language, and use pinned headlines and dynamic elements.

5. Ignoring Quick-Fix Variables

Device & demographic - these “boring” levers can be some of the quickest wins in accounts with limited budget.

6. Over-Indexing on Brand

Don't do this. 

7. Weak Competitor Bidding

Competitor campaigns require nuance.

If it’s low search, high LTV - you can do competitor exact match.
If it’s high search, low LTV - use modifiers like “brand + alternative.”

Also, please don’t be lazy with your ad copy (AKA: ‘WE ARE SO MUCH BETTER’). There’s so much potential to do something cool.

8. Discovery & YouTube

Most Discovery spend is wasted (mainly on Peppa Pig). YouTube remarketing, on the other hand, is criminally underused - and it’s one of the strongest tools in the B2B playbook.

9. No Dedicated Landing Pages

I’m not going to say landing pages always convert better, because they don’t. But a lot of times they do. So test them out (I’ve linked a landing page template in the video).

10. Running in Isolation

If you forget about your Google Ads traffic after it’s ‘not converted’ - that’s an issue.

The real results come when it’s combined with LinkedIn Ads and outbound - creating multi-touch journeys and ringfencing intent accounts.

11. Ignoring Bing

If you’re only on Google, you’re leaving money on the table. Bing should be your next step.
```
