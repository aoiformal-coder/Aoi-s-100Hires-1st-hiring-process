I used to believe waiting for more data was the responsible move.

More information. More certainty. Better decision.
Experience changed that.

Some of the most costly decisions I've witnessed weren't bad calls.
They were delayed ones.

A customer relationship that drifted for two quarters while the team waited for the health score to confirm what everyone already sensed.
 
A team restructure that was obvious in month three but happened in month nine.
An escalation that could have been a conversation, but became a crisis.
The goal of leadership isn't certainty.
It's directional clarity.
 
Knowing enough to take the next step.
Knowing who to bring in when more context is needed.
Knowing when the cost of waiting outweighs the cost of being wrong.

The best decisions I've made weren't the most confident ones.
They were the most honest ones — made clearly, with whatever picture I had.

How do you know when you have enough information to act?
